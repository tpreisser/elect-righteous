## Attributed Messaging Inventory

### 2020 Candidate Questionnaire
Source: [Hays Post, "CANDIDATE PROFILE Ellis County Commission: Neal Younger," July 30, 2020](https://hayspost.com/posts/70a84c66-2d84-4023-b4bb-df360cbe90dd)

- Public health decision-making: said commissioners should consult the head of EMS and communicate with the medical center before making community health decisions.
- Economic development: said the county should be more proactive, work with Hays, improve water supply, and pursue "higher-paying jobs."
- Department relations: emphasized more meetings with department heads and communication with employees.
- City-county communication: said city commissioners should attend county meetings and county commissioners should attend city meetings.
- Budget stabilization: said Ellis County should maintain what it has until more normal conditions returned.
- Public service style: said he would talk with taxpayers, attend surrounding city meetings, town meetings, and fire meetings, and get involved.
- Voter concern: identified "Ellis County spending too much money" as a top concern and said people should be kept involved.
- Roads and infrastructure: described road tours and scheduled maintenance for high-volume roads.
- Experience: described himself as an electrician and plumber with small and large construction experience, focused on listening and "common sense."
- County departments: said he would spend one to two hours in county administrative offices and departments to understand staffing needs.
- Employee pay: supported cost-of-living raises and budgeting raises for county employees.
- County attorney staffing: said he would visit the office and evaluate workload before budgeting another position.
