# Barb Wasinger Social Harvest

Candidate: Barb Wasinger (R)  
Office: Kansas House District 111  
Election slug: ks-house-111  
Harvest date: May 11, 2026  
Public date range covered: 2019 through May 11, 2026

## Presence Detector

Barb Wasinger has a light but identifiable candidate-controlled public web presence. The strongest owned sources are two campaign websites: the older/current-data site [barbforkansas.com](https://barbforkansas.com/) and a newer site, [barbwasinger.com](https://barbwasinger.com/), whose page metadata says it was published December 11, 2024 and modified September 10, 2025. The current Kansas Legislature page confirms her official state role, contact information, House service since 2019, and committee assignments through the 2025-2026 session.

Public social presence exists but is thin from an open-web perspective. Her campaign sites link to Facebook and Instagram, and the older campaign site also has Twitter/X and LinkedIn icons. Indexed third-party civic pages identify Facebook as `electbarbwasinger` and Twitter/X as `BarbWasinger`. The older campaign footer includes "Latest Tweets," but no tweets rendered in the accessible HTML. Facebook, Instagram, X, and LinkedIn did not expose reliable post timelines through unauthenticated public fetches.

## Date Range And Source Corpus

The useful public messaging corpus runs from her first House term messaging on [barbforkansas.com](https://barbforkansas.com/) through public quotes and legislative coverage available by May 11, 2026. The harvest includes:

- 2 candidate-controlled campaign websites.
- 1 official Kansas Legislature profile.
- 5 campaign-site social link/account checks.
- 13 public quote/interview/news items with candidate-attributed statements or actions.
- 1 caucus page identifying her as vice-chair of the Kansas Truth Caucus.

Because social timelines were not reliably accessible without login or script execution, this harvest does not treat social engagement as complete.

## Highest Visible Engagement

No reliable native Facebook, Instagram, X, or LinkedIn engagement metrics were publicly visible without login/scripted access. The highest visible public attention signals were therefore off-platform:

- The newer campaign site presents a concise, first-screen quote about "talk less, listen more" and lists eight results-focused accomplishments, suggesting a campaign reset from issue promises toward incumbent results.
- Hays Post's 2024 questionnaire and 2024 election-night coverage are strong local media signals. Wasinger won re-election in 2024 with 7,548 votes to 2,744 for Joseph Lantz in Hays Post's report.
- Kansas Reflector coverage shows high-salience statewide attention around her role in transgender sports/facilities legislation and the 2026 debate-limiting procedural motion.
- Hays Post coverage of HB 2433 and the R9 water dispute marks her most district-specific policy win by 2026.

## Notable Candidate-Controlled Messages

- Newer campaign site: Wasinger says her approach is to "talk less, listen more, and work together to actually get things done," and says she will stand strong for "farmers, ranchers, and hardworking families" and "heartland values." Source: [barbwasinger.com](https://barbwasinger.com/).
- Newer campaign site result list: "Lowered Property, Sales, and Income Taxes," eliminated state taxes on Social Security benefits and groceries, acted on fentanyl and online pornography, funded K-12/special education, championed FHSU, improved Medicaid/foster care, fought overregulation, and defended "Common-Sense Kansas Values." Source: [barbwasinger.com](https://barbwasinger.com/).
- Older campaign promises: schools, health care, foster care, seniors, school safety, APRN/pharmacist practice, emergency limits on governors closing businesses/churches, foster care regulations, food sales tax, retirement taxes, FHSU funding, business regulation, K-12 dollars, and foster parenting rules. Source: [barbforkansas.com](https://barbforkansas.com/).
- Older campaign note: she framed her first term around "getting things done for Ellis County," especially the Northwest Corridor, and said she stood for tax, life, farmers, small businesses, citizens, and reduced regulations. Source: [barbforkansas.com](https://barbforkansas.com/).
- Biography/identity: both campaign sites emphasize Hays, her husband Tom, four children, small business ownership, prior city/county service, and St. Joseph Catholic Church. Sources: [barbwasinger.com](https://barbwasinger.com/) and [barbforkansas.com/about](https://barbforkansas.com/about/).

## Methodology

I checked candidate-controlled campaign domains, social links from those domains, official legislative pages, indexed civic/social profile previews, local Hays Post questionnaires and legislative coffee coverage, Kansas Reflector statewide coverage, HPPR/KCUR coverage, and obvious platform absences. I treated only material that was candidate-controlled, official, or directly candidate-attributed as usable "own words" evidence. When a platform was login-walled, script-only, temporarily blocked, or did not expose post content publicly, I recorded that limitation rather than inferring unseen posts.
