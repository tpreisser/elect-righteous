## Notable Candidate-Attributed Messaging

- 2021 reason for running: Vajnar said he wanted the best possible education for all USD 489 students, including his own children in the district. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 5, 2021](https://hayspost.com/posts/23c8a445-672d-44c2-be79-5d6dc47a2b3a).
- 2021 qualifications: He cited 31 years teaching in USD 489, Kansas Teacher of the Year, National Board Certification, and endorsements in agriculture education, biology, drivers education, and administration. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 5, 2021](https://hayspost.com/posts/23c8a445-672d-44c2-be79-5d6dc47a2b3a).
- 2021 public-education problems: He named teacher shortages and retention, COVID learning loss, curriculum, facility quality, and access to classrooms for educators. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 5, 2021](https://hayspost.com/posts/23c8a445-672d-44c2-be79-5d6dc47a2b3a).
- 2021 core belief: His decision test was whether a decision is best for USD 489 students. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 5, 2021](https://hayspost.com/posts/23c8a445-672d-44c2-be79-5d6dc47a2b3a).
- 2021 facilities/bond stance: He said facilities needed improvement and argued passing a bond issue was critical after decades without one. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 5, 2021](https://hayspost.com/posts/23c8a445-672d-44c2-be79-5d6dc47a2b3a).
- 2023 nepotism debate: Hays Post reported Vajnar and Allen Park objected to hiring the superintendent's wife through routine personnel approval, prompting debate over the district nepotism policy. Source: [Hays Post, Apr. 26, 2023](https://hayspost.com/posts/9fb91da0-f9e5-4d80-a065-c5c2727c6fb8).
- 2023 construction contract: Vajnar voted against approving the Hays High/Roosevelt guaranteed maximum price contract and said he did not believe he had enough opportunity to educate himself on the contract. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 5, 2023](https://hayspost.com/posts/6942435f-2284-422c-9ed9-5bf7d68c4984).
- 2024 iPad/graduation policy: Hays Post reported a graduation-policy revision was tabled because Vajnar wanted a list of electives that would meet the new STEM requirement. Source: [Hays Post, Mar. 5, 2024](https://hayspost.com/posts/4186bd0a-c7d1-4fe4-8622-6b0204fed740).
- 2025 bond management: Vajnar supported looking into another bond-project manager, joining the losing side of a 3-4 vote, while Hays Post also reported his view that hiring one was late in the process. Source: [Hays Post, Mar. 28, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/e4ec7011-734d-4c18-ab28-c5b38d236126).
- 2025 naming rights: Vajnar delayed the Golden Belt Bank naming-rights discussion, saying the district should be in charge of naming rather than reacting and should give supporting businesses an opportunity. Sources: [Hays Post, Aug. 19, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/a90fb672-f7e5-4826-b5f3-1decd214e83c), [Tiger Media Network, Aug. 19, 2025](https://tigermedianet.com/?p=88429).
- 2025 naming-rights policy: Vajnar argued facility names belong to Hays citizens, said the offer was not enough for a 10-year term, and favored earmarking naming-rights revenue for field maintenance. Source: [Hays Post, Sept. 11, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/aefb9f40-2e86-4bb2-976e-2f731e7ce14f).
- 2025 candidate forum: Vajnar supported a full-time grant writer, called naming rights "sponsorships," said the superintendent brings items to the board through a chain of command, and defended discussion among board members with different perspectives. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 7, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/e326ecf7-cf47-4f86-8ab8-57f7169f8536).
- 2025 Q&A: He said he ran again to keep good things going, finish the improved facilities, and continue support for teachers. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 14, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/199257e6-fc97-4089-8a4c-67a87d3422f8).
- 2025 Q&A budget/class-size message: He described teacher raises, taxes, fixed-income residents, and class sizes as tradeoffs the board has to weigh. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 14, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/199257e6-fc97-4089-8a4c-67a87d3422f8).
- 2025 Q&A safety message: He said he helped push for the 2023-24 school resource officer and would continue pushing for a second SRO. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 14, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/199257e6-fc97-4089-8a4c-67a87d3422f8).
- 2025 Q&A technology/transportation: He backed safer vehicle leasing for long trips and said the board researched the laptop decision for high-school students rather than accepting the first recommendation. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 14, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/199257e6-fc97-4089-8a4c-67a87d3422f8).
- 2025 assessment discussion: As board president, Vajnar said he hoped curriculum would focus on real-world math rather than teaching to the test. Source: [Hays Post, Nov. 14, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/a1ad62ca-72ac-46d4-a3c1-e2248ae736b3).
- 2025 sponsorship committee: Hays Post reported Vajnar said committee members suggested three- to five-year sponsorship contracts rather than lifetime contracts. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 31, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/0330d54b-d2ee-4dcc-a4d5-995fd0f53307).
