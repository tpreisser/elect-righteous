## Platform Notes

### Facebook

Public metadata confirms a page called [Sandy Jacobs - Hays City Commission](https://www.facebook.com/people/Sandy-Jacobs-Hays-City-Commission/100063604999773/) with 257 likes. GoodParty links the older page URL, which redirects to the same Facebook identity. The page description says Jacobs took on the city commission challenge because of her belief and passion for Hays, but the logged-out page did not expose a usable post feed, dates, comments, shares, or post-level reactions. No Facebook post text was counted as reviewed.

### LinkedIn

The public LinkedIn preview for [Sandy Jacobs](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sandy-jacobs-64a43613) showed Hays, Kansas, 246 followers, 244 connections, City of Hays experience, and biographical language about being a retired bank executive leading a nonprofit foundation while serving as a city commissioner. Because LinkedIn blocked direct fetch and only preview snippets were visible, I treated this as identity/background evidence and did not treat liked posts as candidate messaging.

### Official City Pages And Meeting Archive

The [City of Hays City Commission page](https://www.haysusa.com/269/City-Commission) lists Jacobs as a commissioner and links agendas, minutes, live meetings, recorded meetings, and audio. It states that Hays uses a commission-manager form of government and that the commission is a policy-making body. The [agenda center](https://www.haysusa.com/AgendaCenter) showed city commission packets, minutes, and YouTube links through May 7, 2026. I used meeting coverage and agenda/archive presence as public civic evidence, not as social content.

### GoodParty

[GoodParty](https://goodparty.org/candidate/sandy-jacobs/hays-city-commission) lists the profile as unclaimed, identifies Jacobs as nonpartisan, gives the office as Hays City Commission, gives the election date as Nov. 4, 2025, and links Facebook plus the city page. The profile says the candidate has not filled out occupation or top issues, so it was excluded from issue counts except as platform-presence evidence.

## Methodology

I searched public web results, the City of Hays commission page and agenda archive, GoodParty, Facebook metadata, LinkedIn public previews, Hays Post, Tiger Media Network, Kansas Reflector/Lawrence Times, JC Post/Hays Post, and targeted searches for X/Twitter, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Bluesky, Threads, Truth Social, Gab, Gettr, Substack, Medium, and Reddit. I used candidate-controlled sources where available and candidate-attributed public quotes where social feeds were absent or inaccessible. Login-walled, robot-blocked, script-blocked, or preview-only material was recorded as a limitation and was not used for post-level topic or engagement claims. Topic counts are based on accessible items through May 11, 2026 and are non-exclusive.
