## Current Role / Campaign Verification

The official Ellis County Attorney page states that the office primarily handles misdemeanor and felony criminal prosecution, alcohol/drug care and treatment cases, child-in-need-of-care matters, traffic violations, juvenile offender cases, and mental illness matters. It lists Aaron J. Cunningham as Ellis County Attorney from 2025 to present. ([Ellis County](https://www.ellisco.net/92/Attorney))

Hays Post's June 4, 2024 candidate filing coverage listed "Aaron J Cunningham" as the Republican candidate for Ellis County Attorney. ([Hays Post](https://hayspost.com/posts/71fc45b0-c8c1-41af-87da-6b86c8a8170b)) IKE Lab's 2024 general-election results show Aaron J. Cunningham (R) receiving 11,379 votes, with 146 write-ins. ([IKE Lab](https://www.ike-lab.com/complete_elex_2024_gen_cotwp_Ellis.html))

The existing project `campaignWebsite` value for this candidate points to `https://mann.house.gov/about`, which is an unrelated congressional page. No campaign website was found or used.

## Public Platform Signals

Cunningham's public footprint is thin on campaign social media and stronger in official/professional pages and attributed news coverage.

His official office page frames the county attorney's work around enforcement of Kansas law, public safety, truth-seeking, fair and impartial justice, prosecution efficiency, taxpayer cost control, and a priority order that begins with deaths, child victims, child-in-need-of-care cases, sex crimes, violent person crimes, property crimes, and drug distribution crimes. ([Ellis County](https://www.ellisco.net/92/Attorney))

His clearest policy interview is the February 17, 2025 Hays Post profile after he took office. He said he returned to Hays to serve his hometown, described hundreds of likely backlogged cases, said the office usually had four attorneys handling about 1,500 law-enforcement reports per year, and announced a return to marijuana and hemp-derived THC enforcement under Kansas law. ([Hays Post](https://hayspost.com/posts/7ccb3a8f-a8c6-4baa-9767-cf155c00f4a1))

That same interview gives the main public tension in his message: strict law enforcement, but limited resources and some space for treatment. He said the Legislature decides what is criminal and that he must enforce the law as written; he also said a person stopped with only a marijuana pipe on the interstate might not be prosecuted if not an imminent local threat. He said Recovery Court, treatment, and mental-health care have value and that meeting defendants has shown him the need for a holistic approach. ([Hays Post](https://hayspost.com/posts/7ccb3a8f-a8c6-4baa-9767-cf155c00f4a1))

The about.me page is the most personal candidate-controlled profile found. It presents him in explicitly Christian and Calvinist terms, lists Hays and Manhattan, Kansas, Hays High and Kansas State University, and links X/Twitter handle `Sinfonian24601`. The profile's text is not a campaign platform and appears older than his county-attorney term, but it is still publicly visible and self-presented. ([about.me](https://about.me/aaron.cunningham))

LinkedIn is present but limited. The public preview identifies him with Kansas State University and Washburn University School of Law and shows 246 followers and 245 connections. It did not expose a public post timeline in logged-out capture, and direct fetch was blocked. ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/aaron-cunningham-653b3094))

Earlier public biography from the Hays High Guidon in 2019 matches the later local-service theme. Cunningham told students he was drawn to law by advocacy and a strong sense of justice, described servant-hearted practice as a useful skill, and said the goal of his job was to correct poor behavior and help victims. ([The Guidon](https://hayshighguidon.com/news/2019/10/26/assistant-ellis-county-attorney-aaron-cunningham-speaks-to-students-about-career-in-law/))

Public legal/civic presence also appears in the Kansas Rural Justice Initiative. Kansas Courts lists Aaron Cunningham, attorney, Hays, on the committee roster, and the final report search result identifies him as a newer-attorney panelist in Dodge City during the committee's listening sessions. ([Kansas Courts](https://kscourts.gov/About-the-Courts/Court-Administration/Committees/Kansas-Rural-Justice-Initiative-Committee), [final report PDF](https://www.kscourts.gov/KSCourts/media/KsCourts/court%20administration/Rural-Justice-Initiative/Kansas-Rural-Justice-Initiative-Committee-Final-Report.pdf))

Public case coverage shows him emphasizing systems, victims, and consequences. In March 2026 sentencing coverage, Hays Post reported that Cunningham argued against probation in a criminal-threat/aggravated-assault case and warned that vigilante action would signal people could bypass established investigative and court systems. ([Hays Post](https://hayspost.com/posts/a5f579fa-6c33-44fd-921e-5a8435208d58))

He also appears in public safety and victim-focused community contexts. HaysMed listed him as a panelist in a June 27, 2025 human-trafficking awareness luncheon with HaysMed, police, and a retired KBI agent. ([HaysMed](https://www.haysmed.com/haysmed-panel-sheds-light-on-human-trafficking-at-chamber-luncheon/)) KWCH reported in February 2026 that Cunningham confirmed a second-degree murder conviction in an infant methamphetamine-overdose case. ([KWCH](https://www.kwch.com/2026/02/11/father-convicted-murder-approaching-2-years-after-hays-infants-fatal-meth-overdose/))

National third-party attention comes from the Kristen Trickle murder case, where CBS/48 Hours identified him as assistant Ellis County attorney and quoted him in the program's case narrative. This is not campaign messaging, but it is a high-visibility attributed professional appearance. ([CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/kristen-trickle-colby-trickle-kansas-psychological-autopsy-criminal-case-to-determine-mindset-of-fatal-shooting-victim-48-hours/))

## Methodology

Date range covered: October 26, 2019 through May 11, 2026. The start date is the earliest accessible public, candidate-attributed professional message reviewed; the endpoint is the capture date. Role-specific campaign/office coverage begins in 2024 and continues through current public sources available by May 11, 2026.

Platforms and source classes surveyed: Ellis County official pages and staff directory, Kansas attorney registration, Ellis County Bar Association, KCDAA, about.me, LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, Bluesky, Threads, Truth Social, Gab, Gettr, Substack, Medium, Reddit, Hays Post, KWCH, CBS/48 Hours, Salina Post, HaysMed, FHSU, Kansas Courts, Phi Alpha Delta, and broad public web search.

Items examined: 19 public items or source classes, including 15 accessible message-bearing or metadata-bearing items, 2 direct social checks with script/login limitations, and 2 indexed professional pages that were not fully fetchable. Login-walled, script-blocked, robot-blocked, or otherwise inaccessible social feeds were not treated as reviewed content. Engagement is reported only where visible and attributable in public capture.

Disclaimer: This is a public, logged-out harvest of candidate-controlled or candidate-attributed messaging plus official records and credible public coverage. It reports visible patterns and documented absences in the capture set; it does not infer private views, unobserved social activity, or inaccessible account content.
