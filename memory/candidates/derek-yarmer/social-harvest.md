## Candidate-Controlled Or Attributed Statements

### 2021 USD 489 Vacancy Application

In a 2021 application letter published by Hays Post, Yarmer wrote that educating children is "one of the most important responsibilities we have as parents" and said he wanted to be active in the education of his daughters and other students in the district. He also presented his medical background as directly relevant to pandemic-era school decisions, saying his "front-line healthcare" experience and access to medical literature could help the district take a pragmatic approach to protecting students and staff while minimizing interruptions to education.

The same letter cited his post-secondary and medical training as a different perspective for the board and said his construction-industry experience could help with infrastructure decisions.

Source: [Hays Post, Sept. 15, 2021](https://hayspost.com/posts/f743144f-86f5-409f-af1d-729253115b28)

### 2023 Candidate Questionnaire

In the 2023 Hays Post candidate questionnaire, Yarmer described himself as a 39-year-old ER doctor with children in USD 489 schools. His campaign financing answer was: "My campaign is 100 percent personally financed."

Recurring themes in the questionnaire:

- Better facilities, but with cost control: "I want to help make sure that the Hays residents get the new schools they were promised at the price they were promised."
- Board transparency: he said all school board meetings should be broadcast, agendas should be released at least a week in advance, and board members should be able to add agenda items easily.
- Board role: he said the board should oversee "the broad goals of the district, how money is spent, and what students are taught," while not managing day-to-day operations.
- Bond oversight: he said the board has "the absolute responsibility" to keep the project on budget and deliver the structures promised to the community.

Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 23, 2023](https://hayspost.com/posts/0ef676f6-e0ff-4a1c-b697-d9f74d3d7649)

### 2025 Written Statement On Bond And Capital Outlay Spending

Hays Post published a Yarmer email statement in full after asking him to elaborate on concerns that the community was not receiving what residents expected from the bond projects. Yarmer argued that the district was using capital outlay money for items that should have been covered by the bond and criticized priorities including a concession stand, scoreboards, tennis courts, and a marquee sign while roofing and classroom work remained deferred.

Key attributed messages:

- He said capital outlay was "meant to maintain our facilities" and should not be used for things he believed belonged under the bond.
- He wrote that "the school's administration has failed to follow through on its promise to the taxpayers of USD 489 regarding its use of the bond money."
- He wrote that the school board shared blame for "reckless spending" because of insufficient oversight.

Source: [Hays Post, Mar. 28, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/52e53c90-5182-42f0-919e-2c7dc145305b)

## Professional Public Messaging

HaysMed's provider page identifies Yarmer as an Emergency Department physician, with medical school at the University of Kansas School of Medicine and emergency medicine residency at the University of Missouri-Kansas City.

Pawnee Valley Community Hospital's 2023 announcement of Yarmer as Emergency Department medical director includes his public statements about rural emergency care. He said PVCH was "well-equipped to care for patients" and said he hoped to be a resource by offering "guidance and support when needed."
