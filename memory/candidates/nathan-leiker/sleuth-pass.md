## Observations (issue-mapped)

### Observation 1 — Voted NO on Fairview Patch final plat (Jul 1, 2025)

**What was observed:** Leiker cast the lone NO vote on the final plat for the Fairview Patch subdivision. The motion passed 2-1 with Berges and Younger voting yes. He had previously raised concerns about the same plat in May 2025, saying its location worked against the comprehensive plan, and in early July saying it was "getting far from localized subdivision patterns."

**Verbatim where available:**
- "It is getting far from localized subdivision patterns." — Leiker, Hays Post coverage of the Fairview Patch hearing, July 2025.

**Sources:**
- [Ellis County Commission Minutes — July 1, 2025](https://www.ellisco.net/AgendaCenter/ViewFile/Minutes/_07012025-681) — primary
- [Hays Post — Fairview Patch final plat (Jul 1, 2025)](https://hayspost.com/posts/fa81e374-6f44-4875-8984-b7869e10c11d) — secondary
- [Hays Post — Fairview Patch preliminary plat (May 6, 2025)](https://hayspost.com/posts/4f685bbb-15b3-4649-acc2-fae5a0efb0bd) — secondary

**Issue mapping — "County infrastructure / roads & subdivisions / rural land use":** This is the clearest observable on-record divergence from his two colleagues during the term. It maps to subdivision/planning governance, not to ag policy or to budget. Locks to the issue because (a) it is a roll-call action specifically about subdivision approval, and (b) Leiker grounded his stated position in the comprehensive plan rather than in private-property rhetoric.

**Preliminary source tier:** primary (county minutes) + secondary (Hays Post).

### Observation 2 — Supported 1.96-mill increase for 2026 budget after department-by-department review

**What was observed:** Leiker voted yes on the 2026 budget that raised the county mill levy by 1.96 mills. He publicly framed the increase as a "soft landing" cushion against possible federal/state grant withdrawal and specifically credited the commission's nearly 13-hour line-by-line departmental review as the justification.

**Verbatim:**
- "It at least allows us the flexibility to be able to have a soft landing when we have federal or state grants pulled." — Leiker, Hays Post, Aug 14, 2025.
- "We spent almost 13 hours going through each department, item by item, justifying costs and adjustments." — Leiker, Hays Post, Jan 13, 2026.

**Sources:**
- [Hays Post — 2026 budget draft / mill levy discussion (Aug 14, 2025)](https://hayspost.com/posts/7c57b3c8-1314-4bd7-8501-f559ff7c176d) — secondary
- [Hays Post — 2026 budget approved (Aug 2025)](https://hayspost.com/posts/0e698b7b-a28f-4a21-9b40-880346e893ec) — secondary
- [Hays Post — 2026 salary adjustments (Jan 13, 2026)](https://hayspost.com/posts/18d99844-46b7-4bc0-a558-9d9a87fa92e6) — secondary

**Issue mapping — "Ellis County budget / mill levy / taxes":** Direct, on-record fiscal action. The observation is notable because it pairs a mill-levy increase (which would typically draw fiscal-conservative criticism) with a public process argument — the line-by-line review — rather than an ideological frame.

**Preliminary source tier:** secondary, well-triangulated across three Hays Post pieces.

### Observation 3 — Public expression of skepticism toward commission's own zoning competence; sought outside consultant on solar review

**What was observed:** During Ellis County's Tallgrass Development solar project review (Jan 2026), Leiker explicitly stated the commissioners were not experts and pushed for outside consulting help via AcreStrong. He questioned the credentials of AcreStrong managing partner Oleg Alba before agreeing to the engagement. He also called for pausing further zoning certificate approvals to reevaluate the regulatory framework.

**Verbatim:**
- "The three of us as commissioners recognize that we're not experts in doing this and we're looking for expert help to guide us on the best decision for our constituents." — Leiker, Hays Post, Jan 13, 2026.
- "I thought now would be a good time to pause and reevaluate some of the topics that we've seen arise in the past several zoning certificates that have been awarded." — Leiker, Hays Post, Jan 13, 2026.

**Sources:**
- [Hays Post — 2026 salary/solar/reorganization (Jan 13, 2026)](https://hayspost.com/posts/18d99844-46b7-4bc0-a558-9d9a87fa92e6) — secondary
- [Hays Post — bond projects / Tallgrass review agreements (Feb 3, 2026)](https://hayspost.com/posts/7ad9fcb9-1308-464e-8cf4-d3de9e986b40) — secondary
- [Hays Post — plat approval process update (Mar 3, 2026)](https://hayspost.com/posts/d4ad7eb6-4af1-419c-b0ec-e8786acdca3d) — secondary

**Issue mapping — "County personnel decisions / outside expertise / process transparency":** Maps to two issue working titles at once — (a) commission process / hiring decisions (engaging AcreStrong), and (b) meeting transparency / regulatory framework review. Locks to "process" rather than to "energy policy" because his observable behavior is about *how* the commission decides, not about whether solar is good or bad for the county. He has not, in the reviewed record, taken a substantive pro- or anti-solar position.

**Preliminary source tier:** secondary, triangulated across three Hays Post pieces spanning two months.

In the same January 2026 reorganization, he accepted the commission's appointment to the High Plains Mental Health board for 2026. (Younger used very similar phrasing in the same coverage; the framing appears to have been a shared commission position rather than a Leiker original, but he is one of the two officeholders the public attribution is anchored to.)

**Verbatim:**

**Sources:**

### Observation 5 — On Big Creek Estates RHID: publicly conditional, not reflexively pro-development

**What was observed:** When the commission considered the reinvestment housing incentive district (RHID) for Big Creek Estates Third Addition (June 3, 2025), Leiker asked specifically about the cost differential between special assessments and an RHID, then said publicly he was not committed to supporting the district yet — that he would "be doing more homework on it." He has separately backed the housing development itself in earlier discussions (April–May 2025), so the conditional posture sits inside a generally pro-housing stance.

**Verbatim:**
- "I have no problem seeing this start advancing forward, but I'm not sure if I'll be able to support it after the public hearing. I will be doing more homework on it." — Leiker, June 3, 2025 commission meeting per Hays Post.

**Sources:**
- [Ellis County Minutes — June 3, 2025](https://www.ellisco.net/AgendaCenter/ViewFile/Minutes/_06032025-676) — primary (image PDF; vote record exists but not fetched as text in this environment)
- [Hays Post — RHID for Big Creek Estates](https://hayspost.com/posts/f77c1ad9-bc7d-4028-9135-866e1584176c) — secondary
- [Hays Post — preliminary plat](https://hayspost.com/posts/6ba197c2-04b6-4e73-8650-431120cea10f) — secondary

**Issue mapping — "Ellis County budget / mill levy / taxes" AND "Ag policy + rural land use":** Maps to fiscal incentives (RHID is a tax tool) more than to pure subdivision policy. Notable because his earliest 2022 Q&A language emphasized "government should have very little authority over what people do on private property apart from illegal actions" — yet his observable behavior on Big Creek and Fairview shows him taking process-and-comprehensive-plan positions, not pure-property-rights positions. That is the kind of stated-vs-behavior comparison narrative-writer should weigh.

**Preliminary source tier:** secondary; primary vote in the June 3 minutes.
