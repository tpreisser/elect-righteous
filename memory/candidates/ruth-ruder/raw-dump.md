## 3. KANSAS BOARD OF REGENTS (GOVERNING BODY FOR FHSU)

### No Election -- Governor-Appointed Positions

The Kansas Board of Regents is **NOT elected**. Its 9 members are **appointed by the Governor** and confirmed by the Kansas Senate. Key facts:

- One member appointed from each Congressional district; remaining at-large
- No more than 5 may be of the same political party
- No two members may reside in the same county
- Terms are 4 years, staggered, expiring June 30

**Relevance to Hays voters:** The Board of Regents governs Fort Hays State University and recently approved the affiliation of FHSU with North Central Kansas Technical College (NCK Tech) and Northwest Kansas Technical College. There is no direct ballot action available, but voters can influence appointments through their state senator (Senate confirmation) and governor's office (appointment).

## 6. STATEWIDE BALLOT MEASURES WITH EDUCATION IMPLICATIONS (2026)

### A. Kansas Supreme Court Justices Amendment -- AUGUST 4, 2026

**What it does:** Would abolish the Kansas Supreme Court Nominating Commission and replace it with direct election of Supreme Court justices in staggered 6-year terms.

**Education connection:** The Kansas Supreme Court has been central to education funding litigation (Gannon v. State, etc.) that resulted in billions of additional education funding. Opponents (including ACLU of Kansas) argue this amendment is retaliation for court decisions on education and reproductive freedom. Supporters argue it increases democratic accountability.

**This is on the August 4, 2026 primary ballot -- not November.**

**Source:** [Ballotpedia: Kansas Supreme Court Justices Amendment (August 2026)](https://ballotpedia.org/Kansas_Elections_for_Supreme_Court_Justices_Amendment_(August_2026))

### B. Kansas Citizenship Voting Requirement Amendment -- NOVEMBER 3, 2026

**What it does:** Would amend the state constitution to explicitly require U.S. citizenship to vote. Passed the House 90-28 and Senate 37-3.

**Education connection:** Indirect. Could affect voter eligibility for future school board elections (though non-citizens are already prohibited from voting under existing law).

**Source:** [Ballotpedia: Kansas Citizenship Voting Requirement Amendment (2026)](https://ballotpedia.org/Kansas_Citizenship_Voting_Requirement_Amendment_(2026))

### C. Kansas SB 75 -- Education Opportunity Tax Credit (LEGISLATIVE, NOT ON BALLOT)

**What it is:** Would provide a refundable tax credit of up to $8,000/child for accredited private school tuition or $4,000/child for non-accredited private school/homeschool expenses. $125M initial funding cap.

**Status:** Introduced by Sen. Renee Erickson (R-Wichita). Received hearings but has NOT passed as of March 2026. This is a legislative action, not a ballot measure -- but it is the most significant education policy fight in the Kansas legislature right now.

**Source:** [Kansas Legislature: SB 75](https://www.kslegislature.gov/li/b2025_26/measures/sb75/)
