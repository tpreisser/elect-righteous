The same statement also touches "Board VP role-specific behavior" (procedural framing of board scope) and "USD 489 governance / board process."

### Issue: Public comment process / board civility / VP role behavior

**Observation R-2 — On-record floor remarks directed at fellow board member during repeated dissent.**

Verbatim, after Allen Park cast a lone "no" vote on an asbestos removal change order: *"Every week we come here and we get these no votes on things that should not be voted no on just to [satisfy] the taxpayer to say, 'I didn't vote no on it.' You have a right to vote the way you want, but I am sick and tired of coming here every week and there's a no vote on something. That isn't right for the public."*

- **Source:** Hays Post, "Board member calls out fellow member after repeated no votes," https://hayspost.com/posts/03620484-d421-4ae2-b3be-d87652788a17 — tier: secondary.
- **Issue mapping:** Primary mapping is "Board VP role-specific behavior" — this is the VP publicly addressing a fellow board member's vote pattern from the dais. Secondary mapping is "Public comment process / transparency" because the framing ("isn't right for the public") characterizes dissent-on-record as a disservice to constituents, which is a procedural claim about how a board member should relate to the public. Also relevant to "Superintendent oversight" by implication, since the underlying votes were on administration-recommended items.
- **Caveat for narrative-writer:** Record the behavior (public floor critique of a dissenting peer) without imputing motive. The remark is observable; whether it indicates impatience, factional discipline, or board-culture concern is interpretive and out of scope here.
- **Preliminary tier:** High-value. Verbatim, dated to a specific motion. **Tier 1.**

### Issue: District land use / property decisions

**Observation R-4 — Two distinct, time-separated on-record positions on the Felten Elementary / district-land sale.**

Two captured floor statements on the same general property question, separated by approximately three months:

(a) December 2025 — *"Board member Ruth Ruder expressed concern that someone might purchase the land for a tax deduction, only to leave it vacant."*
- **Source:** Hays Post, 2025 land-sale coverage, https://hayspost.com/posts/fc95a5ee-b8b3-4d06-b876-7cba2008d1cb — tier: secondary.

(b) March 2026 — On reconsideration of the proposed sale of 7.3 acres north of Felten Elementary: *"If we sit on the land, we get nothing,"* and *"I don't think we can just say no,"* urging the board to invite bidders to a meeting to discuss their projects further and referencing community-housing demand and potential residential-tax revenue.
- **Source:** Hays Post, March 2026 land-sale reconsideration, https://hayspost.com/posts/13cbc154-8d45-445c-8557-acc25fb643e5 — tier: secondary.

- **Issue mapping:** "District land use / property decisions." Distinct enough from "Bond projects / facilities" to warrant its own issue card — different statutory authority (land disposition vs. capital construction), different stakeholder set (bidders, neighbors, housing market).
- **Note for narrative-writer:** These are two observations of the same board member articulating different posture on what is arguably the same underlying transaction at different stages — initially cautioning against speculative buyers, later pressing the board not to "sit on" land. Record both, in order, with dates. Do not characterize this as "flip" or "contradiction" — that is interpretive and the public framing in March is consistent with the December framing if read as "be cautious about the buyer's intent, but don't refuse to engage with bidders." Let readers see the sequence.
- **Preliminary tier:** Tier 1. Verbatim, dated, multiple distinct quotes on a single defined transaction.

Yarmer's "I don't think you give up your parental rights between 8 and 3" statement in the emergency-policy revision context). Secondary mapping is "USD 489 governance / board process" — the third quote articulates a board-priority preference (academic outcomes over facility-design culture issues). The first quote is a direct procedural statement of position on a specific live operational question.
- **Sensitivity flag:** The original Hays High bathroom design (private vs. group / "gender-neutral" framing per earlier Sentinel coverage at https://sentinelksmo.org/hays-high-school-contstruction-project-restrooms-stirs-controversy/) has culture-war valence. The April 22, 2026 closure was triggered by vandalism; Ruth's statements are on the closure decision and on the priority-setting question, not on the original design. Narrative-writer should keep that distinction clear. Do not collapse the two into one "she opposes gender-neutral bathrooms" reading — the closure decision is operationally separable from the design decision, and her quoted reasoning does not address the design.
