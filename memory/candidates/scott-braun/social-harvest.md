# Scott Braun Social Harvest

## Scope

- Candidate/profile: Scott J. Braun, Republican, Ellis County Sheriff.
- Election context: `ellis-county-current`.
- Public date range reviewed: October 31, 2019 through May 11, 2026.
- Harvest focus: candidate/profile-controlled pages, official office channels, public civic profiles, local news quoting Braun, legislative/public-meeting references, and visible absences.
- Important source correction: `elliscountysheriff.com` is the Ellis County, Texas sheriff site and identifies Sheriff Brad Norman in Waxahachie, Texas. It was not used for Scott Braun. The official Ellis County, Kansas sheriff page is on `ellisco.net`.

## Notable Quotes And Remarks

- In his 2019 candidacy announcement, Braun framed his sheriff campaign around "Dedication, Experience and Integrity" after describing his long law-enforcement career, Rotary service, St. Nicholas of Myra Church Council role, Knights of Columbus membership, and professional boards. Source: [Hays Post archive, Oct. 31, 2019](https://archive.hayspost.com/2019/10/31/undersheriff-braun-announces-run-for-ellis-county-sheriff-in-2020/).
- On drug forfeiture, Braun said KSA's position was that forfeiture helps "cutting off their funding mechanism," and warned that losing forfeiture money would affect taxpayers and drug enforcement equipment. Source: [Hays Post, Jan. 11, 2024](https://hayspost.com/posts/5e9ec24b-53c4-40ff-9c9d-36bfbdf8808a).
- On mental health, he said every Kansas sheriff is struggling with mental-health issues and questioned whether people with serious mental illness should be in county facilities. Source: [Hays Post, Jan. 11, 2024](https://hayspost.com/posts/5e9ec24b-53c4-40ff-9c9d-36bfbdf8808a).
- On jail capacity, he said the proposed jail is not something he wants personally: "We need a jail. I don't want it. We need it..." Source: [Hays Post, July 11, 2024](https://hayspost.com/posts/3f35aea3-a505-43de-a463-78d6976e129d).
- On the sheriff app, he said the office needed a direct way to push information to the community, especially during road and weather emergencies. Source: [Hays Post, Jan. 6, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/4de7954a-f04e-4832-9c84-46b4aaf24657).
- On scam calls, Braun said the sheriff's office will not call, text, or email people demanding money or personal information. Source: [Hays Post, Mar. 14, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/df831461-e829-4c10-8fc1-2e905e69de88).
- After visiting the Arizona-Mexico border, Braun said a secure border is critical to stopping trafficking of narcotics and human beings and tied fentanyl and trafficking threats back to Kansas communities. Source: [Sen. Roger Marshall release, May 31, 2025](https://www.marshall.senate.gov/newsroom/press-releases/senator-marshall-visits-southern-border-alongside-kansas-law-enforcement-officials/).
- On the ICE memorandum, Braun emphasized that Ellis County cannot hold someone beyond the legal detainer window and that deputies are not stopping people on the street for immigration investigations. Source: [Hays Post, Nov. 20, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/3bc2ad3c-f94d-4a73-a42b-77f18e6317d6).
- In HB 2771 testimony coverage, Braun framed the bill as liability clarity, fiscal responsibility, and taxpayer protection, while also saying community safety remained the main reason for staying in the ICE program. Source: [Hays Post, Mar. 6, 2026](https://hayspost.com/posts/69131eb8-04ac-4b1d-9561-e24c9309ad07).

## Visible Absences

- No verified campaign website was found. The apparent `elliscountysheriff.com` source is a Texas sheriff site and should not be used for Braun.
- No public personal/campaign Facebook page was verified separate from the official office page.
- No verified Instagram, Threads, TikTok, or YouTube candidate/profile account was found.
- The official Facebook and X pages are office channels, not visibly campaign channels in the accessible source set.
- Facebook and X did not provide accessible post archives through this environment, so platform post frequency, comments, shares, and likes were not harvested.
- Braun's public messaging is concentrated in office operations, law enforcement association leadership, and quoted news/meeting coverage rather than campaign-style platform pages.
- No public statements found in the reviewed source set on abortion, school curriculum, LGBTQ issues, election administration, or most non-law-enforcement state policy.

## Methodology

I searched the web for Scott Braun, Scott J. Braun, Ellis County Sheriff, Ellis County KS Sheriff, the county-linked Facebook handle `EllisCoSheriff.KS`, the county-linked X handle `ELCoSheriffKS`, Kansas Sheriffs' Association officer listings, Hays Post coverage, Kansas Legislature/HB 2771 records, local public-meeting records, and civic-profile references. I prioritized official county pages, public source pages controlled by or directly tied to the sheriff's office, attributed quotes, and public legislative/news records. I did not fabricate inaccessible social posts; where Facebook, X, or legislature PDFs were blocked or unavailable, this harvest says so and relies on public previews, official link evidence, and accessible reporting.
