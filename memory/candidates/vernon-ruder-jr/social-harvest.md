## Notable Candidate-Attributed Messaging

- 2020 candidate profile: Ruder said he ran after noticing turnover and spending issues in the treasurer's office and said he could decrease spending and increase efficiencies. Source: [Hays Post, July 31, 2020](https://hayspost.com/posts/a828fae7-451a-45b3-aed9-e12596bf13f4).
- 2020 landfill/management pitch: Ruder said he ran the landfill like his own money and said he knew he could decrease spending by thousands in the treasurer department. Source: [Hays Post, July 31, 2020](https://hayspost.com/posts/a828fae7-451a-45b3-aed9-e12596bf13f4).
- 2020 efficiency ideas: Ruder proposed a kiosk for after-hours tag and tax payments and an app to tell visitors office wait times. Source: [Hays Post, July 31, 2020](https://hayspost.com/posts/a828fae7-451a-45b3-aed9-e12596bf13f4).
- 2020 transparency pitch: Ruder said he would present a monthly financial report showing where county money was and how it was invested. Source: [Hays Post, July 31, 2020](https://hayspost.com/posts/a828fae7-451a-45b3-aed9-e12596bf13f4).
- 2020 working-official pitch: Ruder said he would be there when the doors were open, know every job in the department, and work more than required. Source: [Hays Post, July 31, 2020](https://hayspost.com/posts/a828fae7-451a-45b3-aed9-e12596bf13f4).
- 2020 professionalism pitch: Ruder said he solves problems, does not create conflict, and wanted professionalism in the office. Source: [Hays Post, July 31, 2020](https://hayspost.com/posts/a828fae7-451a-45b3-aed9-e12596bf13f4).
- 2020 signed letter: Ruder described himself as bringing a fresh eye and 30-plus years of private-sector knowledge to move Ellis County forward. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 13, 2020](https://hayspost.com/posts/3a069c69-9317-434a-8454-26da7a61d342).
- 2020 signed letter: Ruder said he grew up in Hays, raised his family there, graduated from Thomas More Prep and NCK-Tech, and gave up financial-advisor travel because family was most important. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 13, 2020](https://hayspost.com/posts/3a069c69-9317-434a-8454-26da7a61d342).
- 2020 signed letter: Ruder said he was running to ensure sound investments of taxpayer money, decrease spending, restore trust and professionalism, increase efficiency, work full time, and improve accuracy/ease of transactions without increased taxpayer cost. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 13, 2020](https://hayspost.com/posts/3a069c69-9317-434a-8454-26da7a61d342).
- 2023 motor-vehicle fee: Ruder said Kansas allows county treasurers to help fund motor-vehicle operations with a facility fee, and that the state no longer buys the office's computers or printers. Source: [Hays Post, Nov. 20, 2023](https://hayspost.com/posts/637d35a7-f280-4818-bef9-6bd7f8d30494).
- 2023 tax-burden framing: Ruder said motor-vehicle operations should be funded with motor-vehicle money and estimated the added fee would generate more than $50,000 while helping reduce the treasury budget from $452,000 in 2022 to $367,000 in 2023. Source: [Hays Post, Nov. 20, 2023](https://hayspost.com/posts/637d35a7-f280-4818-bef9-6bd7f8d30494).
- 2024 TMP voter event: Ruder told students voting could make a difference in the county and city by putting people in office who can do a good job for them. Source: [Hays Post, Feb. 22, 2024](https://hayspost.com/posts/2cb7cdcf-1199-4696-8356-6849a960b247).
- 2024 Hays Middle School voter event: Ruder recommended that students get involved in their community and local politics early. Source: [Hays Post, Sept. 30, 2024](https://hayspost.com/posts/fd6be90b-bcb4-49d7-bf9a-20194a876c3c).
- Current official office page: Ellis County describes the treasurer office as responsible for tax billing, collection, and distribution, county monies, and motor-vehicle titling/registration for Ellis County residents. Source: [Ellis County Treasury](https://www.ellisco.net/566/Treasury).
