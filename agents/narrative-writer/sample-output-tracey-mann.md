# In His Own Words — Rep. Tracey Mann (R, KS-01)

*A summary of what Tracey Mann has publicly said, posted, and amplified across social media. Drawn from public posts on X, Facebook, YouTube, and Instagram between August 2024 and April 2026. We report patterns; we do not interpret intent.*

---

Across the public record on X, Facebook, and YouTube, Mann's most frequent topic is agriculture policy. He posts about the Farm Bill, commodity producers, ag trade promotion, and the operational mechanics of U.S. food aid. On January 7, 2026, he posted: ["Producers like @wheatworld are right: As a crown jewel of American soft diplomacy and generosity, Food for Peace is best handled by @USDA. I introduced H.R. 1207, which permanently moves Food for Peace to USDA for the good of our producers and worldwide consumers."](https://x.com/RepMann/status/2008919330285560095) His House floor video ["Rep. Mann Lays Out His Priorities for 2026 on House Floor"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N6TltE1cU-k) opens with farm policy, as does his March 7, 2026 video ["'It Is So Critical': Tracey Mann Calls For Passage Of 'Farm Bill 2.0'"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WnijVyuY9TQ) and the shorter [Farm Bill 2.0 priorities clip](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/IyitozNaNs8). On March 10, 2026, he posted about [Farm Bill 2.0 listening sessions across the district](https://x.com/repmann/status/2031457525825212825). The single most-tagged account across his posts is @POTUS (approximately 23 mentions), followed by @SecRollins (approximately 14) and @wheatworld (approximately 9).

Mann's public alignment network is consistently Republican federal and Kansas-delegation accounts. He tags @POTUS, @HouseAgGOP, @HouseGOP, @SenateGOP, @SecRollins, and @USTradeRep at a steady cadence. On the Food for Peace bill, his House cosponsor is Rep. Derek Schmidt (R-KS), who posted on his own account: ["Happy to be a cosponsor of @RepMann legislation to permanently move the Food for Peace program to USDA. In my view, that's where it always has belonged. There's good progress, and I'm optimistic we can finish the job."](https://x.com/RepDerekSchmidt/status/2011478885175599463) Sen. Jerry Moran (R-KS) is identified as the Senate companion-bill partner on the same measure. On July 2025 farmer aid, Mann [tagged the President, Secretary Rollins, and the U.S. Trade Representative in a single post of thanks](https://x.com/RepMann/status/1948802989944078553).

Engagement on Mann's content is not evenly distributed across topics. His October 14, 2025 border-security Facebook post drew [2,900 reactions, 343 comments, and 79 shares](https://www.facebook.com/TraceyMannKS/posts/kansans-were-less-safe-under-president-bidens-leadership-securing-the-border-isn/1194494539166059/), which is an order of magnitude above his farm-policy posts on the same page. The post reads in full: "Kansans were less safe under President Biden's leadership. Securing the border isn't optional — it's essential to keeping Kansas families safe. President Trump is stopping illegal immigration, taking down drug cartels, and putting America first. Show your support for strong borders and safer communities. Do you support securing the border?" By comparison, a June 20, 2025 KAKE News post quoting Mann on U.S. strikes on Iran drew [328 reactions, 260 comments, and 15 shares](https://www.facebook.com/KAKEnews/posts/republican-us-rep-tracey-mann-of-kansas-said-friday-the-nation-ought-to-get-behi/1188428359993525/), with visible dissent in the comment thread, including one commenter who wrote: "People voted for him because we didn't want to be involved in a war on the other side of the world."

On national and foreign-policy questions, Mann's posting cadence is lower than on agriculture but consistent in direction. In November 2024 he [posted condemning antisemitic violence against Israeli soccer fans in Amsterdam](https://x.com/RepMann/status/1854887766452064340). In June 2025, as quoted by KAKE News, he said the country "ought to get behind" the President's decision on strikes against Iran. In October 2025 he [posted in support of the Israel-Gaza ceasefire and hostage deal](https://x.com/RepMann/status/1978957123934453904) and, separately, [assigned responsibility for the federal government shutdown to congressional Democrats](https://x.com/RepMann/status/1980393593992015903). In July 2025 he [posted on his vote for the One Big Beautiful Bill Act](https://x.com/RepMann/status/1940891252146557141). In February 2026 he [posted in support of the affordability framing in the President's State of the Union address](https://x.com/RepMann/status/2026645022242677156), and in April 2026 he [posted on repealing the federal estate tax, which he refers to as the death tax](https://x.com/RepMann/status/2039702590045163853).

For the KS-01 district itself, Mann's local content centers on agriculture and on in-person constituent contact. In August 2024 he [posted a tally citing 246 town halls and a 60-county Mann Listening Tour](https://x.com/RepMann/status/1826719239861919945). His March 2026 Farm Bill 2.0 listening-session post described district stops by county. His June 11, 2025 YouTube video shows him [questioning Agriculture Secretary Rollins on ag trade promotion and global food aid](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=05W1liBjLCs), framed around Kansas producers. A December 17, 2025 [AgriTalk podcast appearance](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dX5K5BBYnGA) follows the same district-and-commodity pattern. A separate account of Mann's town-hall practice, written from an oppositional viewpoint, is [published on the Indivisible MHK Substack](https://indivisiblemhk.substack.com/p/tracey-manns-town-halls); readers can compare it against Mann's own count.

Several policy areas that affect KS-01 households do not appear at meaningful frequency in Mann's public feeds during the harvested period. Healthcare access, Medicaid, Medicare, prescription drug pricing, hospital closures, mental-health services, K–12 education funding, higher-education affordability, housing costs, child care, opioids, veterans' services, and water policy (including the Ogallala Aquifer) are either absent or appear only as single passing references. Discussion of social or family-life topics — marriage, divorce, abortion specifics, foster care, adoption — is also not present in the harvested set. The dominant local frame is producer-facing rather than household-facing.

Mann's platform footprint is concentrated. He maintains an [official Facebook page with 5,413 likes](https://www.facebook.com/RepTraceyMann) and a [campaign Facebook page with 5,012 likes](https://www.facebook.com/TraceyMannKS), an active X account [@RepMann](https://x.com/RepMann) alongside a dormant alternate @TraceyMannKS, a [YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCGYSHqv8uxVdej-GDuvXCbw) with floor speeches, committee clips, and podcast appearances, and an [Instagram account @reptraceymann](https://www.instagram.com/reptraceymann/) with 4,319 followers, 155 following, and 1,357 posts. His [LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/tracey-mann-34a653bb/) lists 367 connections and a "President — 3 Strands Neighborhoods" headline, with no detectable public activity. He does not have a detectable account on Reddit, TikTok, Truth Social, Gab, Gettr, or Substack, and there is no personal email newsletter found at harvest time.
