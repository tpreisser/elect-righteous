# In His Own Words — Pat Proctor (R, Kansas Secretary of State candidate)

*A summary of what Pat Proctor has publicly said, posted, and amplified across social media. Drawn from public posts and indexed snippets on X, Facebook, YouTube, and Instagram between April 2025 and May 2026. We report patterns; we do not interpret intent.*

---

Across the harvested public record on X ([@RealPatProctor](https://x.com/RealPatProctor)), Facebook ([@PatProctorKS](https://www.facebook.com/PatProctorKS/)), YouTube ([Pat Proctor for Kansas](https://www.youtube.com/@patproctorforkansas655)), and Instagram ([@patproctor4ks](https://www.instagram.com/patproctor4ks/)), Proctor's dominant topic is election administration. The same phrase recurs across every platform: his Instagram bio reads "Fighting to restore confidence in our elections," his Facebook page lists him as "fighting to restore confidence in our elections," and his April 2025 campaign-launch [YouTube video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8oGPhVyuYfs) opens with the line "Job one is restoring confidence in our elections." A second recurring frame is foreign money in Kansas elections. On May 6, 2025, Proctor posted on X: "The axis of ballot harvesting strikes again! Loudlight, Appleseed, and their allies hide behind their supposed nonpartisan, nonprofit status to rake in millions from George Soros and foreign billionaires that they use to sue every common sense measure we pass to restore…" ([source](https://x.com/RealPatProctor)). On May 27, 2025, he posted on X: "Why are the @aclukansas, Planned Parenthood, and the other members of the pro-abortion 'Kansans for Constitutional Freedom' suing to protect their ability to hide behind their nonprofit, supposedly 'non-partisan' status to rake in millions from foreign billionaires" ([source](https://x.com/RealPatProctor/status/1927386363806073212)).

Proctor's public alignment network on his own feed centers on Kansas Republican officeholders, election-policy organizations, and his own legislative work product. His Facebook page features a campaign video titled ["Kris Kobach Wanted This 20-Year-Old To Go To Jail For…"](https://www.facebook.com/PatProctorKS/videos/enforcing-our-election-laws/1920239725529443/) framing his own enforcement stance against Attorney General Kris Kobach's. A separate page post identifies him as "the chairman of your House Elections Committee and as your next Secretary of State" ([source](https://www.facebook.com/PatProctorKS/posts/as-the-chairman-of-your-house-elections-committee-and-as-your-next-secretary-of-/1227794369167664/)). An October 2025 [YouTube video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HsDMhN6Ltbs) records his endorsement of Steve Clay for Leavenworth City Commission, the only down-ballot endorsement visible in the harvested set. The harvested set contains no posts amplifying, tagging, or appearing alongside his Republican primary opponent for Secretary of State, Rep. Ken Rahjes.

Engagement on Proctor's content is not evenly distributed. His Facebook page hosts the highest-engagement videos when they touch enforcement of election law and ballot-integrity claims, including the [Kobach-framed enforcement video](https://www.facebook.com/PatProctorKS/videos/enforcing-our-election-laws/1920239725529443/). Critical engagement is also concentrated there: the activist account [Loud Light](https://www.facebook.com/LoudLightKS) reposts and challenges his statements at video-length, including a clip titled ["Rep. Pat Proctor after being confronted at a town hall, the police are on their way"](https://www.facebook.com/LoudLightKS/videos/rep-pat-proctor-after-being-confronted-at-a-town-hall-the-police-are-on-their-wa/1516695455979360/) (captured from his April 19, 2025 Leavenworth town hall) and a second video questioning a [statement about Kansas voters](https://www.facebook.com/LoudLightKS/videos/have-you-all-heard-pat-proctor-for-kansas-secretary-of-state-latest-lie-when-he-/628564576673155/). At that April 19, 2025 town hall, asked by a constituent to explain remarks about women voters, Proctor replied, "That'd be really terrible if I'd actually said that" ([source](https://kansasreflector.com/2025/06/13/leavenworth-house-member-first-republican-to-declare-as-2026-candidate-for-secretary-of-state/)).

On national and federal-policy questions, Proctor's feed engages selectively and through a state-elections lens. He frames opposition to federal mail-in-ballot deadlines, election-related litigation, and nonprofit-funded ballot measures as state sovereignty questions. On April 11, 2025 his House-carried [HB 2106 foreign-funding ban](https://news.ballotpedia.org/2025/04/11/indiana-joins-kansas-kentucky-and-wyoming-in-enacting-laws-to-restrict-foreign-contributions-to-ballot-measure-campaigns-the-highest-number-passed-in-a-single-year/) was enacted, and he posted on the bill's significance from his X account. In an April 2025 fundraising email, Proctor described Kansas Reflector as "part of a vast, dark money, left-wing propaganda machine called States Newsroom" and stated that "a significant percentage of that organization's funding" comes from foreign sources, including Swiss businessman Hansjörg Wyss ([reported by Kansas Reflector](https://kansasreflector.com/2025/06/13/leavenworth-house-member-first-republican-to-declare-as-2026-candidate-for-secretary-of-state/)). States Newsroom states that it follows all laws regarding nonprofits and does not accept donations from foreign-based entities.

For the Leavenworth district itself, Proctor's local content is less voluminous than his statewide-elections content but is present. An October 5, 2025 [YouTube video](https://www.youtube.com/@patproctorforkansas655) addresses bringing jobs and opportunity back to Leavenworth. His campaign and legislative biographies foreground his co-ownership with his wife Aree of Baan Thai Restaurant in Leavenworth. The April 2025 [Wichita State University](https://www.kslegislature.gov/li/b2025_26/members/rep_proctor_pat_1/) listing identifies him as a college professor in addition to his House role. The harvested set does not contain Leavenworth-specific posts about Fort Leavenworth military-family issues, federal-prison employment, or city infrastructure at a frequency comparable to his statewide election-administration posts.

Several policy areas affecting Kansas households do not appear at meaningful frequency in Proctor's harvested public feeds during the captured period. Healthcare access, Medicaid, hospital closures, K–12 education funding, agricultural commodity policy, the Ogallala Aquifer, child care, opioids, and veterans' services other than his own veteran identification are either absent or appear only as single passing references. The captured period contains no posts addressing his Republican primary opponent for Secretary of State, Rep. Ken Rahjes. The dominant frame is election administration, election security, and the funding sources of organizations that have sued the State of Kansas over election law.

Proctor's platform footprint is moderately distributed. He maintains a campaign Facebook page at [@PatProctorKS](https://www.facebook.com/PatProctorKS/), an X account at [@RealPatProctor](https://x.com/RealPatProctor), a YouTube channel at [@patproctorforkansas655](https://www.youtube.com/@patproctorforkansas655), an Instagram account at [@patproctor4ks](https://www.instagram.com/patproctor4ks/), and a LinkedIn profile at [pat-proctor-phd-b477575](https://www.linkedin.com/in/pat-proctor-phd-b477575/) listing his Kansas State House role and PhD. He does not have a detectable account on Reddit, TikTok, Truth Social, Gab, Gettr, or Substack identified at harvest time.

---

**Methodology:** Date range covered: April 2025 to May 2026. Platforms surveyed via direct visit where accessible and via Google indexed snippets where login-walled: X (@RealPatProctor), Facebook (PatProctorKS), YouTube (@patproctorforkansas655), Instagram (@patproctor4ks), LinkedIn. Posts examined: approximately 30 indexed posts across platforms plus campaign launch and committee-chair videos. Reposts examined: limited indexed sample. Engagement counts captured at time of harvest where visible; precise follower/like counts not captured because Facebook, X, and Instagram blocked direct profile fetch during this harvest window. Quotes are verbatim from indexed sources; links lead to source. We report patterns; we do not interpret intent.
