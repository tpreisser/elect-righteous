# PART II: SECRETARY OF STATE RACE (Open Seat -- Scott Schwab term-limited/running for Governor)

**Why this matters**: The Kansas Secretary of State is the state's top election administrator. This office controls how elections are run, who gets to vote, and how votes are counted. After years of national controversy over election integrity claims, this race is critically important.

### Basic Profile

  - 2009: Operations officer for Task Force Patriot (2nd Battalion, 32nd Field Artillery) in Tikrit, Iraq

### Education

### Published Books

- Co-author: *ASVAB AFQT Cram Plan*

### Legislative Record -- ELECTION BILLS (This is the critical section)

**As House Elections Committee Chairman, Proctor has been the primary legislative driver of election restriction bills in Kansas:**

**HB 2437**: Mandates state agencies that register voters (DMV, public assistance) share personal information including Social Security numbers with the Secretary of State's office. Requires cross-referencing driver's license records and voter rolls against the federal SAVE database twice yearly. Restricts voter registration websites to .gov domains or state-approved sites. Requires county officials to remove voters from rolls when funeral homes publish obituaries. Also incorporated HB 2491 language requiring quarterly reports of noncitizens receiving public benefits to the Secretary of State.

**HB 2569**: Could eliminate no-excuse mail-in voting if any judge deems the state's ballot signature verification law invalid. Mandates all voting rights challenges be heard in Shawnee County (where the civil judge has leaned conservative). Passed House 78-45.

**HB 2491**: Requires names, addresses, and personal ID of non-citizens receiving public benefits to be regularly shared with Secretary of State.

**Other votes:**
- Voted to ban ranked-choice voting in Kansas (2025)
- Voted to require early voting ballots be received by 7:00 PM on Election Day (2025)
- Voted to override Gov. Kelly's veto of early voting restriction (one of 84 Republicans)

### "Axis of Ballot Harvesting" -- Connection to Election Conspiracy Theories

**THIS IS A MAJOR RED FLAG.**

Proctor coined the phrase "axis of ballot harvesting" as a catch-all term for anyone who challenges his claims about early voting. The Kansas Reflector reported this phrase "serves as a catchall for anyone who challenges his false claims about the hazards of early voting, and signals a vast make-believe conspiracy by foreign nationals to undermine elections."

When three nonprofits filed a lawsuit challenging new restrictions on early voting, Proctor posted on social media: *"The axis of ballot harvesting strikes again!"*

**When asked by Kansas Reflector to clarify his statements, Proctor responded:**
> "Here's a quote for you: 'You are a partisan hack and your so-called "newspaper" is a woke, leftist propaganda rag.' Thanks for the opportunity to comment."

The Kansas Reflector specifically noted: *"Proctor has been among the Legislature's advocates for election reform bills associated with President Donald Trump's unproven assertion his 2020 loss to Democrat Joe Biden was fraudulent."*

### Campaign Platform for Secretary of State
- Increase voter confidence
- Increase transparency
- Ensure only citizens vote in Kansas elections
- "Restore confidence in the voting system"

### ACLU Opposition
The ACLU of Kansas has called Proctor's election bills an "extreme attack on voting by eligible citizens" and warned of "death by a thousand cuts to Kansans' right to vote." They note that documentary proof-of-citizenship requirements previously blocked 35,000 eligible Kansas voters from registering between 2013-2016 under former Secretary of State Kris Kobach.

### Key Assessment
**Proctor is the ideological successor to Kris Kobach in the Secretary of State's office.** He chairs the Elections Committee and has used that position to advance bills that restrict voting access under the banner of "election integrity." His hostile response to press inquiries, his "axis of ballot harvesting" rhetoric, and his alignment with Trump's 2020 election fraud claims make him the most consequential -- and most controversial -- candidate in this race. If elected, expect aggressive voter roll purges, restrictions on mail-in voting, and noncitizen-focused enforcement actions.

# PART III: STRATEGIC ANALYSIS

## Governor's Race -- Democratic Primary

**The race is effectively Corson vs. Holscher.** Tuley is irrelevant.

**Corson's Advantages:**
- Massive fundraising lead ($902K vs. $397K)
- Endorsements from Gov. Kelly, Fmr. Gov. Sebelius, and Rep. Sharice Davids
- "Middle-of-the-road" positioning designed to appeal to independents and moderate Republicans in the general
- Younger (43 vs. 56)
- U.S. Commerce Department experience useful for economic arguments

**Holscher's Advantages:**
- More legislative experience (9 years vs. 5)
- Stronger election results (won re-election by 23 points)
- Rural working-class origin story (tenant farmer grandparents, janitor mother)
- More specific policy positions (especially on school vouchers)
- Polled ahead among decided voters (33% to 9%) -- BUT this was a Holscher-funded poll and 58% were undecided

**Key Dynamics:**
- The Kelly endorsement was controversial. Holscher publicly accused Kelly's staff of trying to clear the field for a different candidate (Chris Mann). Kelly ultimately endorsed Corson, which stung.
- Both candidates are from Johnson County, which is a problem in a state where rural voters are decisive
- 58% of Democrats were undecided in January 2026 -- this race is wide open
- The August 4 primary is the real contest. Whoever wins faces a heavily Republican general electorate.

## Secretary of State -- The Race That Actually Matters

**This is the most consequential downballot race in Kansas in 2026.** The Secretary of State controls how elections are administered. The two Republican candidates represent fundamentally different visions:

- **Proctor** = Kris Kobach 2.0. Aggressive voter restriction, noncitizen-focused enforcement, conspiracy-adjacent rhetoric, hostile to press and voting rights groups. If elected, expect lawsuits, voter roll purges, and national controversy.
- **Rahjes** = Traditional Republican administrator. Business-focused, pragmatic about elections, less ideological. Would likely maintain the status quo on voting access while improving business services.

The Republican primary winner likely wins the general in Kansas. **The Proctor vs. Rahjes primary is therefore the most important election on the Kansas ballot in 2026.**

On the Democratic side, Jennifer Day is the obvious frontrunner over Sam Lane, but the Democratic nominee faces long odds in the general election in this red state.
