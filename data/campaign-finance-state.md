# Kansas 2026 Campaign Finance -- State Candidates

**Last Updated**: 2026-04-01
**Data Period**: Year-end 2025 filings (reported January 2026) unless otherwise noted
**Kansas Contribution Limit**: $2,000 per individual per election (primary + general = $4,000 total cycle); no limit on PAC-to-PAC transfers

---

## HOW TO ACCESS RAW DONOR DATA

Kansas does NOT make it easy. The news articles name almost no individual donors. To get actual names and amounts, you must go to these databases:

1. **Kansas Itemized Contributions Database** (searchable): https://www.kansas.gov/campaign-finance/statewide/itemized/index
2. **Kansas Public Disclosure Commission Contributor Data**: https://kpdc.kansas.gov/campaign-finance/campaign-contributor-data/
3. **Kansas Secretary of State Campaign Finance Viewer**: https://kssos.org/elections/cfr_viewer/cfr_examiner_entry.aspx
4. **Scanned PDF Reports (2026 Cycle)**: https://www.kansas.gov/ethics/CFAScanned/StWide/2026ElecCycle/SWLinks2026EC.htm
5. **FollowTheMoney.org**: Data for 2026 cycle NOT YET LOADED (typically lags until after election cycle closes)

**CRITICAL NOTE**: The scanned PDFs contain Schedule A (itemized contributions) with full donor names, addresses, employers, and amounts. These are the gold mine. Direct PDF links for key candidates are listed below where found.

**Donor Analysis**:

- The timing shift suggests a deliberate pivot to MAGA donor networks

- Total Masterson ecosystem: ~$1.4M ($700K campaign + $700K dark money)

**INVESTIGATIVE FLAG**: Take Back Kansas is the biggest dark money story in this race. $700K from undisclosed donors in a nonprofit controlled by the sitting Senate President. Who is funding it? Koch network? Out-of-state conservative groups? Fossil fuel interests? This is a prime target for investigation. As a 501(c)(4), it is not required to file with the Kansas Ethics Commission, but IRS Form 990 may eventually reveal some information.

**Also notable**: Masterson dropped Axiom Strategies (Jeff Roe's firm, major national GOP consultancy based in KC) to hire "Trump-aligned" consultants. This suggests a strategic shift toward the MAGA base and potentially different donor pools.
