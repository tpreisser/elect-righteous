## 6. LEGISLATIVE RECORD & MAJOR BILLS

### A. FLAT TAX / INCOME TAX REFORM

**SB 169 (2023):** Proposed single flat income tax rate of 4.75% for all Kansans earning above $5,225/year. Estimated cost: $566 million/year. Part of three-pronged approach: (1) zero tax on healthy food, (2) reduce/eliminate Social Security tax, (3) flat income tax.

**SB 539 (2024):** Would change income tax rates over six years, starting at 5.7% in 2024 and reducing by 0.05% annually to 5.45% by 2029. Governor Kelly vetoed.

**2025-2026:** Led legislature to garner 2/3 majority support for reducing personal income tax to a single rate of 4%. Framed as part of a $2 billion cumulative tax reduction.

Masterson's stated position: "We're building a movement of everyday Kansans who are fed up with being taxed to death and watching their state fall behind."

### B. PROPERTY TAX

**Constitutional Amendment (CAP):** Pushed a constitutional amendment to cap annual property assessed value increases at 3%. Passed the Senate but failed to achieve 2/3 majority in the House (March 2026).

**Quote:** "Property taxes are squeezing Kansas families, seniors on fixed incomes, and farmers. Kansans expect action, not excuses. I will keep fighting to deliver real relief and I urge the House to put taxpayers first."

Failed in 2025 session as well -- Senate fell one vote short of approving the constitutional amendment on property taxes (March 26, 2025).

### C. TRANSGENDER LEGISLATION

**Transgender Sports Ban (2023):** Overrode Governor Kelly's veto (3rd consecutive year Kelly vetoed this legislation). House 84-40, Senate 28-12. Masterson statement: "While we were hopeful the governor would meet us in the middle and honor her campaign rhetoric, we stood firm on our commitment to fairness for Kansas women."

**Gender-Affirming Care Ban for Minors (2025):** Legislature overrode governor's veto (February 18, 2025). Masterson statement: "By any reasonable standard, governing from the middle of the road should include ensuring vulnerable children do not become victims of woke culture run amok."

**Bathroom Bill / SB 244 (2026):** Forces transgender people to use bathrooms matching biological sex at birth in government buildings. Governor Kelly vetoed on February 13, 2026, citing "numerous and significant consequences." Senate overrode veto February 17-18, 2026. Masterson statements:
- "Sadly, our governor has decided she will side with they/them over simple, scientific truth."
- "Kansans need not worry -- the Kansas Senate will restore sanity, and override her veto."
- "SB 244 restores sanity in Kansas."
- "We swiftly overrode Laura Kelly's radical veto that would have forced our mothers, sisters, wives, and daughters to share their bathrooms with biological men in government buildings."

### D. DEI BAN

Led the investigation into DEI practices at Kansas universities after receiving a student complaint. Shared the complaint with members of the State Finance Council. Resulted in legislation banning DEI in state government and higher education (signed into law by Governor Kelly in April 2024 -- Kelly allowed it to become law without her signature).

K-State was subsequently found to be in violation of the DEI law in January 2026.

Campaign claim: "Shut down wokeness by banning DEI in our higher education system."

### E. MARIJUANA / CANNABIS

**Position:** Steadfastly opposed to legalization. Called medical marijuana a "nonstarter."

**Key Quotes:**
- Feared legalization would fuel "gang activity" -- cited Oklahoma's experience with medical marijuana
- "If you look at that question, I think most people would answer yes, but they don't know what they're actually saying yes to." (dismissing polls showing 60-70% Kansas support for legalization)
- At January 2026 debate: wouldn't welcome legislation to legalize, but endorsed Trump's decision to open the door to research, predicting it would demonstrate marijuana was "damaging"
- Open to "some type of pilot program or something that is controlled to the point you can test it"
- Will only consider legislation that "protects our children"

**Context:** 70% of Kansans support medical cannabis and 60% support recreational legalization per polling. Kansas is one of the last states without any form of legal cannabis. Masterson has personally ensured marijuana legislation never reaches the Senate floor.

### F. SCHOOL CHOICE / VOUCHERS

**SB 75 (2025):** $125 million voucher program -- $8,000 tax credit per child in accredited private school, $4,000 per child in non-accredited private school or homeschool.

**Position:** Masterson says school choice legislation is "good public policy" that offers parents more options, particularly addressing "concerns about what's taught about gender and sexuality in public schools."

### G. MEDICAID EXPANSION

**Position:** Steadfastly opposed. Called it expanding "the welfare state" rather than helping the "truly needy."

**Quote:** "There is a whole host of reasons not to do it."

Argued federal funds wouldn't save rural hospitals and would "improperly extend health care through Medicaid to able-bodied adults 19 and 64 years of age."

Medicaid expansion amendment failed 12-28 in Senate (February 2025) with all 28 Republicans opposed.

### H. SUPREME COURT JUSTICE SELECTION AMENDMENT

**Long-running effort (2019-2026):**
- 2019: Championed failed resolution for Senate confirmation model
- 2022: Proposed federal model or statewide elections for justices
- 2025: SR 1611 passed -- placing amendment on August 4, 2026 ballot
- Would allow voters to elect Kansas Supreme Court justices rather than current merit-based nomination commission

**Quote:** "Kansas's current system isn't apolitical at all -- it's just politics veiled by a commission."

Supported by Americans for Prosperity and Kansas Policy Institute.

### I. ABORTION / PRO-LIFE LEGISLATION

**Pregnancy Center CARE Act (HB 2635, March 2026):** Protects private nonprofit pregnancy centers from regulation on information and services. Governor Kelly vetoed; Legislature overrode 87-35 (House) and 30-9 (Senate) on March 27, 2026.

**Masterson quote:** "Good legislation that saves lives."

Joint statement with House Speaker Hawkins: "Common sense measures that support women and babies."

### J. HEALTH CARE SHARING MINISTRIES TAX BREAK (SB 368)

Provides tax breaks for people using nontraditional health care sharing ministries (faith-based alternatives to insurance). Governor Kelly vetoed. Override: 87-37 (House), 30-9 (Senate) on March 27, 2026.

This is the bill whose override prompted the bell-ringing incident.

### K. CELL PHONE BAN IN SCHOOLS

Sponsored SB 302 -- "Bell to Bell, No Cell" policy. Signed into law by Governor Kelly in March 2026. Bans personal electronic devices in all Kansas public schools from start of school day through dismissal starting fall 2026.

**Quote:** "The epidemic of cell phones disrupting classrooms and damaging our kids' mental health is a top priority."

### L. ELECTION INTEGRITY

Supported HB 2138 (2022) -- requires voter-verified paper ballots with distinctive watermark and audits for races within 1% margin.

Spoke at Foundation for Government Accountability event on "Restoring Trust in Elections."

**Campaign claim:** Advanced election security improvements.

### M. REDISTRICTING / CONGRESSIONAL MAP

Pushed for special session in fall 2025 to redraw Kansas congressional maps to disadvantage Democratic Rep. Sharice Davids. Obtained necessary 2/3 Senate signatures for special session call (October 27, 2025). Effort failed in 2025 but Masterson made it a "top priority" for 2026 regular session.

**Quote:** "We'll have no trouble for sure passing a new map with majorities."

### N. GOVERNMENT EFFICIENCY (COGE)

Created the "Committee on Government Efficiency" (COGE) in January 2025, modeled after federal DOGE under Elon Musk. Launched Government Efficiency Portal on legislature website for public submissions.

**Controversy:** Committee restricted access to 1,500+ public submissions until after session. Kansas Reflector independently obtained comments showing "nearly all of the comments were antithetical to the committee's work." Open records complaint filed against the Senate efficiency panel.

### O. VETO OVERRIDE RECORD (GENERAL)

Under Masterson's leadership as Senate President, the Republican supermajority has systematically overridden Governor Kelly's vetoes on major legislation. In April 2025, Masterson convinced the Senate to take a single vote to override three dozen of Kelly's line-item budget vetoes simultaneously (unusual procedure).

## 12. CONTROVERSIES & INCIDENTS

### A. Bell-Ringing Incident (March 27, 2026)

**What happened:** After the House voted 87-37 to override Governor Kelly's veto of SB 368 (health care sharing ministries tax break), Masterson -- who was standing by the House door watching the vote -- walked over to the ceremonial bell outside the House chamber, flipped it to make it ring, and headed back toward the Senate chamber.

**Democratic response:**
- Rep. John Carmichael (D-Wichita) demanded a point of order on decorum violations
- Rep. Kirk Haskins (D-Topeka) criticized "the one that went out into the hall and rang the bell to gloat"
- House Speaker ruled there was nothing in House rules addressing "the issue of the bell outside the House chamber"

**Masterson's response:**
- "No apologies"
- "We won for lower healthcare costs. We will ring that bell every time we put more money back in Kansans' pockets." (posted on X)
- "I look forward to ringing in another override soon."

### B. Hugh Hewitt / Kansas Reflector "Soros" Attack (December 2, 2025)

**Quote:** Masterson told Hugh Hewitt: "We have a kind of a Soros-funded blog that masquerades as the news." (referring to Kansas Reflector)

**Reflector response:** Editor-in-chief Sherman Smith stated: "We have not directly or indirectly received Soros funding, but if someone can put in a good word for us, we would welcome his support in helping us shine a light on what Masterson and others are really up to in the Statehouse."

**Opinion editor Clay Wirestone** published a satirical response comparing Masterson to a raccoon that drank peanut butter whiskey, writing that Masterson "imbibed misinformation and conspiracy theories about the Reflector" and "made a public spectacle of his ignorance about journalism."

### C. Charlie Kirk Resolution Defense (January 28, 2026)

Senate voted along party lines to honor the late Charlie Kirk (assassinated September 2025 at a Utah college). Democrats distributed Kirk's controversial quotes. Masterson erupted:

**Quote:** "What you heard right there were the top three out-of-context, misrepresented, BS remarks that I've heard in the public media."

Specifically defended Kirk's declaration that it was a "huge mistake when we passed the Civil Rights Act in the 1960s" as being taken out of context.

### D. "Democrats Are the Enemy" Debate Statement (January 30, 2026)

After Charlotte O'Hara attacked him for being too friendly with Democrats/Governor Kelly, Masterson gestured at fellow GOP candidates and said: "This is not the enemy. The Democrats are the enemy."

### E. GoCreate Dual Salary Controversy (March 2026)

Democratic Sen. Patrick Schmidt proposed eliminating state funding for the GoCreate director position, arguing Masterson was "drawing two taxpayer-funded salaries." Masterson says he takes unpaid leave during legislative sessions.

### F. Bankruptcy While on Ways & Means Committee

Filed for Chapter 7 bankruptcy (~$885K in unsecured debts) while serving as a member of the Senate Ways & Means Committee -- the committee overseeing the state budget.

### G. COGE Open Records Violations (2025)

The Committee on Government Efficiency restricted public access to submitted comments, redacted names (including Masterson's own name from a submission), and delayed release until after the session. Legal experts said the redactions violated the Kansas Open Records Act. Kansas Reflector filed a formal complaint.

### H. Redistricting / Gerrymandering Effort (2025-2026)

Pushed for special session to redraw congressional maps specifically to disadvantage Democratic Rep. Sharice Davids. Effort failed in 2025 but continued into 2026.
