# Candidate Name

## Who They Are
Write 2 to 4 full paragraphs in plain English. Explain who this person is, where they come from, what jobs or offices they have held, their family or community background when verified, and the details that actually define them in public life. Do not write campaign fluff. Do not write analyst commentary. Use specific names, dates, places, offices, employers, institutions, and projects.

## Their Record
Write 2 to 4 full paragraphs about what they have actually done. This section should cover votes, bills, executive actions, lawsuits, business dealings, controversies, investigations, major projects, leadership roles, or public fights tied to the person. Be concrete. Name the bill, the program, the lawsuit, the project, the committee, the dispute, or the quote that matters.

## What You Should Know
- Write at least 5 bullets.
- Every bullet must be specific and understandable on its own.
- No vague language like "attack line," "liability," "profile," "electability," "corroborates," or "gives opponents an angle."
- Say what happened, who was involved, and why it matters.

## Where They Worship
Write 1 to 2 short paragraphs. If a current church is verified, name the church and include the church URL in the prose. If it is not verified, say that plainly and mention any relevant faith background only if sourced.

## Campaign Finance
Write 1 to 3 short paragraphs summarizing the money trail. Include totals, cash on hand, self-funding, donor patterns, PACs, or the honest statement that no campaign finance data was available online.
